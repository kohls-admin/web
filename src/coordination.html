Coordination Tips

Guidelines for maintaining an active and engaged moderation team.

üéØ Purpose

  • Maintain an active and engaged moderation team.
  • Identify and address issues contributing to inactivity.
  • Foster community and support among staff.

üîÑ Regular Check-ins

  • Frequency: Schedule regular check-ins with your staff, both active and inactive.
  • Well-being: Check in on them and address any concerns.
  • Feedback: Gather feedback on their experience and identify areas for improvement.
  • Support: Offer support, guidance, and resources.
  • Recognition: Acknowledge their contributions and show appreciation.

üîç Identifying Inactive Staff

  • No moderation actions in the past 2 weeks.
  • Minimal participation in staff discussions or public channels.

ü§ù Reaching Out

Initial Contact
  • Direct Message: Send a friendly message expressing appreciation and inquiring about their well-being.
  • Example: "Hi [Name], I hope you're doing well! I noticed you haven't been as active lately, and I wanted to check in and see how you're doing. Is everything okay? Please feel free to share if there's anything I can help with or if you have any questions or concerns."
Follow-up

If no response:

  • After a few days, send a gentle reminder.
  • After two weeks, escalate and notify a Manager.

If they respond:

  • Express understanding and support: Acknowledge any challenges they may be facing and offer assistance or resources if needed.
  • Discuss their role and expectations: Remind them of their responsibilities and the importance of their contributions to our team.
  • Explore options: If they're struggling with time commitments or other issues, discuss potential solutions (e.g., adjusting their role, taking a temporary break).
  • Thank them for their honesty: Express your appreciation for their open communication and reiterate your support.

üí° Additional Tips

  • Create a welcoming and supportive environment.
  • Be proactive: Don't wait for problems to arise before reaching out. Regular check-ins can help prevent issues from escalating.
  • Be flexible: Understand that everyone has different circumstances and commitments. Be willing to work with other staff to find solutions that work for everyone.
  • Show appreciation.

By implementing these strategies, we can create a more engaged and supportive team, leading to a better experience for both staff and the community.

Remember: Be proactive, understanding, and supportive. Foster open communication to ensure our team remains active and committed!
